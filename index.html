Tribute Page:John Garang de Mabior

Dr. John Garang de Mabior

The man who liberated South Sudan

Here's a timeline of John Garang's life

  • 1945: John Garang was born on June 23rd in Buk, Sudan (now South Sudan). He belonged to the Dinka ethnic group.
  • 1962: Garang joined the Sudanese army, starting his military career. He attended the Sudan Military College in Wadi Sayedna and pursued further military education in the United States, Egypt, and Tanzania.
  • 1970: Garang received a scholarship to study at Grinnell College in Iowa, United States, where he obtained a Bachelor's degree in Economics and Political Science. He also earned a Master's degree in Agricultural Economics from Iowa State University.
  • 1972: The Addis Ababa Agreement was signed, ending the First Sudanese Civil War. Garang returned to Sudan and joined the Sudanese army as an officer.
  • 1983: Garang resigned from the Sudanese army and founded the Sudan People's Liberation Movement/Army (SPLM/A). He became the leader of the rebel movement and launched the Second Sudanese Civil War, advocating for the rights of marginalized Sudanese regions and seeking a united and inclusive Sudan.
  • 1985: The SPLM/A and the Sudanese government signed the Addis Ababa Agreement, granting the South Sudan region autonomy. However, the agreement failed to address the root causes of the conflict, leading to renewed hostilities.
  • 1991: A split occurred within the SPLM/A, leading to the formation of the SPLM/A-Nasir faction under Riek Machar. Garang's faction became known as the SPLM/A-Torit, maintaining the original vision of a united and independent Sudan.
  • 2002: Garang and Sudanese President Omar al-Bashir signed the Machakos Protocol, a significant breakthrough in the peace negotiations. The protocol outlined a framework for further discussions and set the stage for the Comprehensive Peace Agreement (CPA).
  • 2005: On January 9th, the Comprehensive Peace Agreement (CPA) was signed, ending the Second Sudanese Civil War. Garang was appointed as the First Vice President of Sudan and President of the semi-autonomous Government of South Sudan. He assumed office on July 9th, marking a significant milestone in Sudanese history.
  • 2005: On July 30th, Garang tragically died in a helicopter crash near the Uganda-Sudan border.

"Our diversity should be a source of strength, not division. Let us embrace our differences and work together towards a united and prosperous Sudan."

--Dr. John Garang de Mabior
